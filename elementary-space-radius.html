Elementary Space Radius

In the SPD framework, the elementary space radius RE is the radius of the elementary space associated with a given particle energy. It defines the scale at which the granular structure of space becomes relevant for that particle.

By comparing RE to the particle's wavelength, one can identify the critical energy at which the wavelength becomes smaller than the elementary space radius — the regime where SPD predicts significant space-curvature effects at the particle scale.

Lengths are in metres (m). Energies are in joules (J). Masses are in kilograms (kg).

The main formula

RE = 2 GE E c4

RE = elementary space radius

GE = elementary space constant = ke e² / mp² = 8.246441821 × 10²⁵ m³ s⁻² kg⁻¹

E = energy of the particle (J)

c = speed of light in vacuum

ke = Coulomb constant

e = elementary charge

mp = proton mass

Wavelength for comparison

To identify the critical energy threshold, the elementary space radius is compared to the particle's wavelength. Two cases apply:

Massless particles (photon, gluon):

λ = hc E

λ = wavelength

h = Planck constant

c = speed of light in vacuum

E = energy of the particle

Particles with mass:

λ = hc √(E² − m²c⁴)

λ = wavelength

h = Planck constant

c = speed of light in vacuum

E = total energy of the particle

m = rest mass of the particle

mc² = rest energy of the particle

Physical constants used

The elementary space constant GE is derived from the Coulomb constant, elementary charge, and proton mass.
