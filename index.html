G-value Calculator

In the SPD framework, the G-value represents the gravitational coupling strength associated with a specific element, isotope, molecule, or material composition.

This calculator allows users to explore and calculate predicted G-value variations for elements, isotopes, molecules, mixed materials, and interacting masses.

All displayed G-values are expressed in units of 10⁻¹¹ m³ kg⁻¹ s⁻².

The main formula

GX = GH × ( npXmp + nnXmn ) / mX

GX = G-value of element X

GH = G-value of hydrogen

npX = number of protons in element X

nnX = number of neutrons in element X

mp = proton mass

mn = neutron mass

mX = inertial mass of element X

How these values are calculated

The values in the lookup tool are based on isotope-weighted inertial masses, isotope-weighted gravitational masses, and the corresponding G-value for each naturally occurring element.

For most elements, the G-value is calculated by dividing the gravitational mass by the inertial mass and multiplying the result by the G-value of hydrogen:

GX = GH × ( gravitational mass / inertial mass )

Hydrogen, iron, and steel are treated as special reference cases. The G-value of hydrogen is inferred from iron, while the G-value of iron is derived from measurements of stainless steel.

Stainless steel: 6.674200 ± 0.000022
Iron: 6.674280 ± 0.000022
Hydrogen: 6.613511

Natural isotope abundance data are based on the CIAAW isotopic abundance reference tables. Representative values are used when isotope abundances are expressed as ranges.

Molecule calculator

The molecule calculator determines the effective SPD G-value of a molecule by first calculating its total inertial mass and total gravitational mass.

Gmolecule = GH × ( Σ gravitational masses / Σ inertial masses )

The inertial mass is obtained by summing the inertial masses of all constituent atoms multiplied by their quantities in the molecule.

The gravitational mass is calculated in the same way using the SPD gravitational masses of the selected elements.

The molecular G-value is then obtained by dividing the total gravitational mass by the total inertial mass and multiplying the result by the G-value of hydrogen.

Effective G-value calculator

When two masses interact, the effective G-value is calculated as the geometric mean of the G-value of the source mass and the G-value of the test mass.

Geffective = √( Gsource × Gtest )
