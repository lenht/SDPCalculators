Time-Redshift Equation

In the SPD entropic expansion framework, this equation gives the light travel distance to any distant galaxy directly from its observed redshift z and the Hubble constant H₀.

Because it is linear in z, the result is exact for all practically observable distances — no numerical integration or cosmological model parameters are required.

Distances are expressed in lightyears (lyr). H₀ is entered in km/s/Mpc.

The main formula

\[t_0 = \dfrac{3\, c\, z}{H_0}\]

t0 = light travel distance to the galaxy

c = speed of light in vacuum

z = observed redshift of the galaxy (dimensionless)

H0 = Hubble constant

The Hubble constant

The value of H0 used here is derived from supernova distance surveys. CMB-derived values depend on Big Bang theory assumptions and are not theory-independent, so they are not appropriate for this framework.

\[H_0 = 74.03 \pm 1.42\ \dfrac{\text{km}}{\text{s}}\ /\ \text{Mpc}\]

For the formula, this converts to metres per second per lightsecond:

\[H_0 = (7.192 \pm 0.142) \times \dfrac{10^{-10}\ \text{m/s}}{\text{ls}}\]

With the default H0, the equation simplifies to:

\[t_0 = 39{,}624{,}161{,}384\ \text{lyr} \times z\]

BAO size & full age of the universe

Using the time-redshift equation, it is possible to calculate both the full age of the universe and the size of Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO). The BAO size is the distance sound waves could travel from the Big Bang until decoupling — the moment photons and matter separated.

The full age follows directly from expressing redshift through the temperature ratio of the CMB at decoupling versus today:

\[t_0 = \left(\dfrac{T_i}{T_0} - 1\right) \times \Delta x_z \quad \text{(full age of the universe)}\]

t0 = full age of the universe (lightyears)

Ti = initial CMB temperature at decoupling (K) — true value: 4,021 K

T0 = current CMB temperature = 2.725 K

Δxz = 3c/H₀ — lightyear-per-unit-z coefficient from the time-redshift equation

The BAO size uses the same bracket, scaled by the sound speed fraction at the time of decoupling, and subtracts the duration of the decoupling epoch td:

\[d_\text{BAO} = \dfrac{\sqrt{\dfrac{5}{3}\,\dfrac{1}{m_p}\,k_B\,T_i}}{c} \times \left[\left(\dfrac{T_i}{T_0} - 1\right)\Delta x_z - t_d\right]\]

Ti = initial CMB temperature at decoupling (K) — true value: 4,021 K

T0 = current CMB temperature = 2.725 K

Δxz = 3c/H₀ — the lightyear-per-unit-z coefficient from the time-redshift equation

td = duration of the decoupling epoch ≈ 4.2 × 10¹³ yr

mp = proton mass

kB = Boltzmann constant

c = speed of light
